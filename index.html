Living Along Data Centers: Investments in AI and Their Impact on Local Communities

This thesis examines how investments in AI affect the communities where supporting infrastructure—specifically data centres—is built. The demand for computing power is steadily increasing, and the locations chosen to host data centres are often selected based on policy incentives and operational efficiency, with limited consideration of local communities and the impacts these facilities may have on them.

Hypotheses

What Was Tested

H0 Positive correlation between household electricity prices and DC share of national energy consumption.
H1 Positive correlation between household electricity prices and DC per capita.
H2 Positive correlation between non-household electricity prices and DC share of national energy consumption.
H3 Positive correlation between non-household electricity prices and DC per capita.
H4 Correlation between ICT sector GVA and data center concentration (energy share metric).
H4b Correlation between ICT sector GVA and DC per capita.
H5 Correlation between ICT employment share and data center concentration.
H6b Correlation between ICT R&D spending (BERD) and DC per capita.
